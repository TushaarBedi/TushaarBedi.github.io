Maximum Temperature

As we go closer to the equator, the temperature rises and as we go up the north pole, the temperature rapidly stars falling down. At around 80-90 degrees latitude, the temperature goes to less than zero! On the left half of the graph, you will see the temperature are still generally warm, even though the distance from the equator is increasing. This is because in the month of March we are still in ‘late winter/early spring’ for the northern hemisphere, which means we are still in summer for the southern hemisphere. If we plot this exact same chart in July or August of this year, we will see that left half of the graph will be touching negative temperatures, because that will be winter for the southern hemisphere (and summer for our norther hemisphere).
